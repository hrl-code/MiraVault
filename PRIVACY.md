# Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-07-03

MiraVault is a local-first desktop media application. It is designed to organize, explore, and play media files stored on your own computer or storage devices.

## Summary

MiraVault does not require an account, does not operate a central user database, does not sell personal data, and does not intentionally collect analytics or tracking data from users.

Most data used by MiraVault stays on your device.

## Data Stored Locally

MiraVault may store local application data on your computer, including:

- App settings and preferences.
- Selected library folders and media sources.
- Viewing progress, watched status, and playback positions.
- Metadata overrides edited by the user.
- Torrent or download settings when those features are enabled.
- Cached metadata, subtitles, or IPTV configuration created by app features.

This data is stored locally in the application's user data directory managed by Electron and your operating system.

## External Requests

Some MiraVault features may contact external services when you use them:

- GitHub may be contacted to check for new MiraVault releases.
- Public metadata providers may be contacted to search for titles, covers, descriptions, ratings, or episode information.
- Subtitle providers may be contacted when searching for subtitles.
- IPTV playlist URLs entered by the user may be requested by the app.
- Torrent, magnet, WebTorrent, or qBittorrent-related features may connect to peers, trackers, local clients, or configured services when used.

These requests are feature-driven and depend on what you configure or open inside the app.

## No Account System

MiraVault does not provide a hosted account system. The project maintainers do not receive your media library, watch history, local paths, IPTV lists, torrent data, or personal files through the app.

## Logs and Diagnostics

MiraVault may produce local logs or technical information to help diagnose problems. These logs remain on your device unless you choose to share them manually, for example when reporting an issue on GitHub.

Do not share logs publicly if they contain private file paths, media names, playlist URLs, or other sensitive information.

## User-Controlled Content

You are responsible for the content, folders, IPTV sources, subtitles, torrents, and links that you add to MiraVault. The application does not provide copyrighted content and does not include media sources, IPTV lists, trackers, or torrent indexes.

## Code Signing and Downloads

Official Windows installer releases are distributed through the MiraVault GitHub Releases page. MiraVault intends to use the SignPath Foundation for code signing of Windows installer releases when available.

## Third-Party Services

External services contacted by MiraVault have their own privacy policies and terms. MiraVault cannot control how those third-party services process requests made to them.

## Changes to This Policy

This policy may be updated as MiraVault evolves. Material changes should be documented in the repository history and release notes.
